Verna Dorian-Alexandru

Student in Computer Science at Politehnica University of Bucharest.
I'm from Reșița, Caraș-Severin, a beautiful city in western Romania, particularly in the mountainous region of Banat.
I come from a family of engineers and I like to think that the idea of following the same path as my parents comes from a desire to know how the inner parts of the things we use all day in our lives work and how these things can be improved in order to make it easier for us to reach new levels of knowledge. I have no problem with living my life in a world where technology has advanced so much that we forget sometimes how life and human relationships used to be a couple of years ago. After all, Computer Science is the domain I have choosen to practice because I believe that all future innovations that are going to happen in the world will rely on computers and their power of processing data and, if I am to be honest, I would like to be a part of what this means. On the other hand, I believe that the moments we spend with our family, friends or in nature are more precious than everything and this is why striking a balance between our working life and these moments represent one of our most important objectives in life.
